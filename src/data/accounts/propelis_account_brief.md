# Propelis Group — Account Knowledge Layer

> **Purpose.** This is the deep-context account brief for a 1:1 conversation with Gary Kohl, CEO of Propelis, and (likely peripherally) Matt Gresge, Executive Chairman. It exists to (a) saturate the meeting prep with verifiable facts, (b) map the integration thesis to specific Cambrian Catalyst engagement vectors, and (c) supply talk tracks, discovery questions, and positioning vocabulary calibrated to a post-merger, PE-backed holding company in year two of integration.

> **Working thesis.** Propelis is a year-two post-merger holding company under HPS (60%) and Matthews International (40%), with five sub-brands, ~$1B revenue, and a stated mandate to "redefine brand delivery from ideation to implementation." Their first 18 months have been brand consolidation (SGS+SGK → SGX, Marks+Equator+SGK Creative → Marks, etc.) and technology re-platforming (5Flow). What they have *not* publicly solved is the commercial integration — unified GTM, cross-brand ICP, account-level orchestration, seller enablement across five businesses with overlapping clients. That is the wedge.

## 2. What makes Propelis distinct

Three things separate Propelis from any other consulting target Cambrian has touched, and they dictate how the engagement is framed:

**1. It is a holding-company-of-services, not a single P&L.** Five operating brands (SGX, Marks, Equator-under-Marks, Collide, 5Flow) sell into largely the same client universe — global CPG, FMCG, pharma, retail — but historically did so as four-plus separate sales organizations. This is the "agency holdco" pattern (WPP, Omnicom, IPG, Publicis), but at ~$1B and freshly post-merger. The single biggest unrealized synergy is *not* cost — it is *commercial orchestration* across the brands into existing accounts.

**2. It is a PE-strategic hybrid, not pure-play PE.** HPS Investment Partners is the majority controller, but Matthews International (NASDAQ: MATW) holds a 40% common stake plus residual preferred equity. This creates a dual-clock dynamic: PE typical 3–5 year hold/exit thinking *plus* a public-company partner who reports its Propelis interest on a one-quarter lag and uses it as a value lever in earnings calls. The implication: EBITDA growth and synergy capture is being narrated to *two* boardrooms quarterly. Visible commercial momentum (pipeline, win-rate, cross-sell metrics) matters for both audiences.

**3. The industry is mid-disruption from AI.** Packaging adaptation, mechanicals, multilingual versioning — the labor-intensive middle of the workflow — is increasingly automatable. Propelis's strategic answer is 5Flow (workflow tech) + Marks (creative platform) + 5Flow's "Ai Lab." That is the right answer technically. The commercial answer — *how the sales force tells that story, prices it, and lands it across 2,000+ clients* — is unsolved. This is where Cambrian inserts.

## 4. Ownership, capital structure, and investor dynamics

### Cap table essentials

- **Matthews International (NASDAQ: MATW)** — 40% common equity + originally $50M preferred + retained $50M AR + $250M cash at formation ($228M net of divested cash). On ~May 13, 2026 announced a $28M *partial* preferred redemption, leaving roughly $22M preferred outstanding. MATW carries its 40% on equity-method accounting and reports financials on a *one-quarter lag*.
- **Joseph Bartolacci, MATW President/CEO**, publicly described Propelis as performing "in line with expectations" and confirmed adj. EBITDA growth + synergy capture as the value-creation narrative.

### Why this cap table matters for the conversation

- **No single board faction owns the long term.** HPS wants exit-multiple expansion; MATW wants quarterly EBITDA performance to show its remaining stake is appreciating; Kohl/Gresge sit between.
- **The preferred redemption (May 2026) is a tell.** Cash is being returned. The business is generating enough free cash to redeem preferred *and* fund integration. This means the EBITDA story is real, not paper. It also means the next 12 months are critical — early returns of capital signal sponsor confidence; further redemptions are the visible scoreboard.
- **The 60/40 governance dynamic** likely means a board with HPS-appointed directors (probably 3–4), Matthews-appointed directors (probably 2), Kohl as CEO, and possibly an independent or two. Gresge as Executive Chairman straddles. Expect operating-partner-style cadence from HPS.
- **BlackRock parentage** is new and probably under-internalized inside Propelis. BlackRock private equity dynamics ≠ pure HPS. Cambrian should not over-index on classical HPS direct-lending behavior; this is a portco transitioning into BlackRock's private credit/PE perimeter.

- **EBITDA:** Adj. EBITDA $32.2M for Jul–Sep 2025 (per MATW preliminary; MATW's 40% share = $12.9M). Run-rate ~$128M; ~13% margin.
- **Cash:** Sufficient for a $28M partial preferred redemption in May 2026 — meaningful liquidity event one year in.
- **Reporting lag:** MATW reports its share on a one-quarter lag due to Propelis "stand-alone reporting systems" transition. **Implication:** Propelis's internal financial systems were still being unified ~18 months after close. That is normal for a JV of this scale, but it tells you their data infrastructure is mid-maturation.

### What this implies for a sales conversation

- **13% margin** is below best-in-class agency holdcos (15–18%) and well below pure-tech (35%+). The EBITDA bridge thesis to PE is *clear*: integration synergies + 5Flow mix shift + commercial cross-sell should push margins toward 16–18% by exit.
- **Top-line growth** is the harder lever — the CPG and packaging industries are barely growing (McKinsey 2026 CPG sales growth ~1.9% organic; volume *declining* 0.5%; private label gaining share). Propelis has to *outgrow the category* via share gain, cross-sell, and tech-attach. This is the GTM execution gap.
- **A 100–200 bp commercial productivity improvement** on a $1B base = $10–20M EBITDA accretion. That is materially meaningful to both shareholders and is *exactly* the lever a GTM/RevOps engagement targets.

## 7. Integration thesis and the value creation map

This is the spine of the conversation. A PE/JV-backed integration creates *exactly four* common value-creation buckets. Cambrian's job is to identify which buckets Kohl is privately worried about and offer the operator wedge.

| Bucket | What it looks like at Propelis | Status | Cambrian fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Cost takeout** | Real estate consolidation; redundant back-office; procurement; production footprint rationalization | Likely 60–80% complete by month 18; visible in EBITDA jump from merger close to current run-rate | Low fit — operational consulting, not Cambrian's lane |
| **2. Tech / workflow modernization** | 5Flow consolidation of Wave, Mediabox, Dragonfly, Photon, Ai Lab; client migration to unified CaaS | Active and being marketed as the proof point | Adjacent fit — could partner with 5Flow GTM rather than compete |
| **3. Commercial integration (cross-sell, ICP unification, unified seller enablement, account orchestration)** | One Propelis client owning *all five* brands instead of one brand per client; pricing across the LOBs; unified ABM into top accounts | **This is the bucket nobody has publicly claimed credit for. It is the visible gap.** | **PRIMARY FIT — this is the Cambrian wedge** |
| **4. Margin mix shift (toward higher-margin services)** | Marks (high-margin creative) and 5Flow (tech/SaaS) growing faster than SGX/Collide (capital-intensive production) | In progress but the GTM motion isn't differentiated by LOB | High fit — Cambrian helps build LOB-specific GTM motions |

### The integration math, articulated

If Propelis has 2,000+ clients and the average client today engages with **1.4 of 5 brands** (a reasonable assumption for a freshly merged business, no public data), and a successful commercial integration moves that to **2.2 of 5 brands**, that is a ~57% increase in revenue per account before considering pricing or new logo. On a $1B base, even a 10–15% net revenue uplift from cross-sell over 24 months = $100–150M topline + materially better margins from operating leverage. **This is the unspoken board narrative.**

Cambrian's role is *not* to do the cross-sell itself. It is to:

1. Diagnose the current cross-sell penetration baseline (no one inside Propelis has time to do this objectively).
2. Build the unified Propelis ICP — which clients/segments are positioned to buy across multiple LOBs.
3. Design the account orchestration model — single account lead vs. brand leads, comp model, governance.
4. Build the seller enablement layer that lets reps in any LOB credibly position adjacent LOBs.
5. Stand up the QBR / pipeline review cadence that surfaces cross-LOB pipeline as a distinct metric.

This is the Cambrian Architecture engagement, scoped for a multi-brand B2B services holdco. It is what BHN/Tango lived through post-acquisition. **It is the precise lived experience Joe brings.**

## 12. Industry and market dynamics

### CPG and packaging sector — the demand side

- **CPG organic growth ~1.9% YoY** (McKinsey, April 2026) with volume *declining* and price/mix carrying the number
- **Private label gaining share** — 80%+ of US/EU consumers now perceive private label quality as equal or superior to branded (McKinsey 2025 survey)
- **SKU rationalization trend** — Reckitt's Dec 2025 sale of Air Wick/Woolite/Easy-Off into "Vestacy" is the visible signal; expect more brand-portfolio pruning across 2026–27
- **Packaging M&A consolidation** — 71 PE container/packaging deals in H1 2026; megamergers from Amcor, Sonoco, IP, Novolex compressing supplier choice
- **Sustainability and EPR regulation** — EU Packaging Waste Regulation provisions mandatory later 2026; clients need design and workflow tooling to adapt at scale

### Agency / brand services — the supply side

- **AI disruption is real but narrow** — Mod Op (Duracell case): AI agents accelerating "labor-intensive middle stages — adaptation, mechanicals, multilingual versions, large SKU counts." Creative concept work is *less* commoditizable. This is precisely the Marks vs. SGX margin dynamic.
- **Speed-to-market is the new battleground** — clients are choosing partners on weeks-to-shelf, not on creative pedigree alone
- **Consolidation in the agency holdco space** — Omnicom-IPG closed late 2025, prompting all other holdcos to defend share

### The implication for Propelis's commercial story

The client buying behavior is:
1. Compress vendor count (fewer partners, deeper relationships)
2. Demand AI-leveraged speed
3. Demand sustainability fluency
4. Demand cross-channel execution (packaging + digital + experiential)

**Propelis is positioned correctly on paper.** Five brands under one parent that can serve packaging, creative, experiential, and tech all at once = exactly what large CPG procurement says it wants. The execution gap is purely commercial — getting clients to *experience* that integration through a single account relationship.

## 13. Technology strategy and 5Flow

### 5Flow's stated capability

- **Wave (CaaS — Content-as-a-Service):** Artwork, design, and packaging workflow platform. Approvals, version control, compliance. Claims 52% faster artwork turnaround.
- **Mediabox:** Asset management adjacency
- **Dragonfly, Photon:** Workflow modules (specific functions less publicly defined)
- **Ai Lab:** AI/ML capabilities; brand-trained agents for adaptation
- **Scale:** 200+ specialists, 140+ global customers, 130,000 users
- **Compliance:** ISO 27001 certified
- **Stated origin:** Started 2011 as Matthews/SGK tech spin-off; expanded via SGS tech consolidation post-merger

### Strategic positioning of 5Flow

- It is **not** sold as a standalone software business in the way that Adobe AEM Assets or Bynder is. It is sold as **the technology that powers a Propelis engagement** plus a la carte to non-Propelis clients (FMCG, pharma, retailers, printers).
- This is the **technology-led services** monetization pattern. The closest analogues are Accenture Industry X, IBM iX, or Deloitte Studio — services-and-software hybrids where software is the differentiator but services capture the margin.
- The GTM challenge: 5Flow's natural buyer (Director of Packaging, VP Brand Operations) is different from Marks's natural buyer (CMO, Brand VP) and SGX's natural buyer (Procurement / Supply Chain). Cross-LOB selling means orchestrating across three different buyer personas in a single account.

### Where Cambrian intersects

5Flow's most likely commercial weakness is **new-logo acquisition outside the Propelis client base**. Most of its 140 customers are SGK/SGS legacy accounts that came over via the merger. To grow as a standalone tech business, 5Flow needs:

- A defined ICP for net-new logo (not just account-based expansion)
- A direct sales motion separate from "ride-along with Marks/SGX account managers"
- A partner / channel motion (systems integrators, packaging consultancies)
- Pricing rigor on standalone CaaS subscriptions vs. embedded-in-service pricing

> See `cambrian_b2b_sales_value_creation.md` for the GTM motion-building framework — 5Flow is closest to the **"embedded SaaS lifting off from a services parent"** archetype.

## 16. Discovery questions for the Kohl meeting

These are calibrated for a 30–45 minute first conversation. The objective is *not* to close. It is to surface the one or two private worries that turn this into a follow-up. Pacing: open with credibility/context, then move to diagnostic, close with a specific micro-commitment.

### Opening / context-building

1. *"Twelve months in — when you look back at the integration so far, what surprised you most? Where did you hit faster than plan, and where did the timeline stretch?"*
2. *"The brand consolidation is largely visible to the market — SGX, Marks, Collide, 5Flow are now distinct narratives. What's the next phase of integration look like internally that the market doesn't see yet?"*

3. *"When a top account engages Propelis today — say a global FMCG with packaging spend in multiple regions — what does their relationship look like across the brands? One account team, or four?"*
4. *"How are you measuring cross-LOB pipeline as a distinct metric? Is that something the LOB Presidents are accountable for, or is it still a holdco-level aspiration?"*

### Talent / execution

7. *"How are you thinking about commercial talent right now? Is the existing seller population well-equipped to sell across the line-of-business model, or is there a gap there?"*

### Investor narrative (later in conversation, with care)

### Close

9. *"If I came back in two weeks with a baseline view on cross-LOB account penetration across your top 25 — built from public data, no client interaction — would that be useful for an internal conversation?"*

## 19. Common failure modes for an account like this

These are the ways a Cambrian engagement with a Propelis-class account *fails*. Each is a defensive note.

1. **Pitching transformation, not orchestration.** Kohl just finished a transformation. Frame the work as *completing* an integration that is largely already done, not as a fresh top-down program.
2. **Over-indexing on consulting jargon.** This is an operator audience. Use operator vocabulary ("seller productivity," "account team," "cross-LOB pipeline") not consulting vocabulary ("capability maturity," "transformation roadmap," "operating model design").
3. **Trying to be the AI expert.** Propelis has 5Flow and the Ai Lab. Cambrian is not the AI consultancy. Stay in the commercial integration lane.
4. **Talking past 5Flow to the LOBs.** Sriram Upadhyayula owns 5Flow and reports to Kohl. If 5Flow GTM comes up, treat it as a peer brand within the holdco, not a side project.
5. **Naming legacy SGS/SGK clients you don't have verified active relationships with.** "I used to work with [client] at BHN" is fine. "[Client] is on the Propelis roster" without verification is a credibility hit.
6. **Pitching too big too early.** A 12-month program is the wrong opening. A 26-week pilot with a $250K–$500K floor is the right opening; a baseline diagnostic with a small fixed-fee scope is the safer opening.
7. **Skipping Gresge.** If the engagement is integration-themed, Gresge owns it on the board. Joe should ask Kohl whether Gresge should be in a follow-up.
8. **Letting the conversation drift to BHN nostalgia.** Use BHN as a credibility frame once, then move on. Joe is not selling BHN; he is selling Cambrian.
