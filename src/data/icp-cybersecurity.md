# ICP — Cybersecurity Playbook

> Vertical playbook for cybersecurity vendors. Distinct ICP dynamics driven by fear-based buying, CISO-committee reality, and a deeply crowded vendor landscape.

## 1. What makes cybersecurity distinct

Five structural features drive ICP work in cyber differently from other B2B SaaS:

**Fear is the primary buying emotion.** Not curiosity, not optimism — fear of breach, fear of regulatory penalty, fear of losing the CISO's job. Messaging that ignores this converts poorly.

**The category is saturated.** The RSA vendor floor has 500+ exhibitors; Gartner's security Hype Cycle tracks 100+ distinct subcategories. Differentiation must be sharp or price-shopping and platform-consolidation pressures dominate.

**Platform consolidation is restructuring buying.** After 15 years of "best-of-breed," CISOs have 60–150 tools and are actively consolidating. Point-solution vendors face existential pressure; platform vendors (Palo Alto, Microsoft, CrowdStrike, Cisco, Zscaler, SentinelOne) have strong tailwinds. ICP must account for which side you're on.

**Validation is everything.** Cybersecurity buyers expect third-party validation: Gartner/Forrester analyst reports, MITRE ATT&CK evaluations, independent lab results (AV-TEST, AV-Comparatives, SE Labs for endpoint; SOC/SIEM/SASE have their own), customer references, certifications.

**Compliance and insurance drive a lot of spend.** A meaningful share of cyber purchases exists to satisfy cyber insurance requirements, regulatory expectations, or audit findings — not to actively reduce risk. ICP should recognize which buying mode the prospect is in.

## 2. Cyber is multiple markets

Subdivisions matter for ICP work:

**By category (simplified Gartner taxonomy):**
- **Endpoint** — EPP, EDR, XDR, MDR, antivirus.
- **Network** — firewalls, NDR, DDoS, DNS security, SASE, SSE, ZTNA.
- **Identity** — IAM, IGA, PAM, MFA, passwordless, identity threat detection (ITDR).
- **Data** — DLP, DSPM, encryption, tokenization, data security.
- **Application** — AppSec, SAST, DAST, ASPM, API security, WAF, RASP.
- **Cloud** — CSPM, CWPP, CNAPP, cloud identity.
- **SIEM / SOAR / XDR** — detection and response platforms.
- **GRC / TPRM** — governance, risk, compliance, third-party risk.
- **Threat intelligence & threat hunting**.
- **Email security** — ATP, phishing, BEC.
- **Security awareness training**.
- **Deception / honeypots / active defense**.
- **OT/IoT security**.
- **MDR / MSSP** — managed services.

**By buyer segment:**
- **Enterprise ($1B+)** — large SOC, dedicated CISO, platform-oriented.
- **Upper mid-market ($250M–$1B)** — dedicated CISO or VP IT + security lead, platform-curious.
- **Mid-market ($50M–$250M)** — IT-led security, limited dedicated staff, often MDR-buying.
- **SMB (<$50M)** — MSP-delivered, bundled, rarely direct buyer.

**By vertical (where compliance drives buying):**
- **Financial services** — FFIEC, NYDFS Part 500, SEC cyber rules, PCI-DSS.
- **Healthcare** — HIPAA, HITRUST.
- **Critical infrastructure** — NERC CIP, TSA pipeline rules, EO 14028, NIST SP 800-series.
- **Government / defense** — FedRAMP, StateRAMP, CMMC, FISMA.
- **Public companies** — SEC cyber disclosure, SOX.
- **Retail** — PCI, state breach notification.

## 3. ICP patterns

Useful dimensions:

- **Security maturity** — defensive posture (CIS Controls level, NIST CSF maturity, zero-trust posture), headcount of security team, SOC presence, mean time to detect/respond metrics.
- **Firmographic** — revenue, employee count, vertical, geography, regulatory exposure.
- **Technographic** — current security stack (endpoint vendor, SIEM, identity provider, cloud vendor), existing platform commitments (Microsoft E5, Palo Alto, CrowdStrike), IT/OT posture.
- **Buying posture** — renewal-driven, incident-driven, compliance-driven, consolidation-driven. Each implies a different sales approach.
- **Cyber insurance status** — does insurance drive any requirements? Recent renewal experience?
- **Recent events** — any known breach, ransomware, regulatory action, M&A?
- **CISO profile** — technical vs. risk/business-oriented; new vs. established; reporting to CIO/CTO/CFO/CEO.

## 4. The buying committee

Cybersecurity committees can be large:

- **Economic buyer** — CISO or CIO for most deals; CFO for large deals; Board/Audit Committee for material spend.
- **Champion** — Deputy CISO, Security Director, SOC Manager, Security Architect.
- **Technical evaluator** — SOC analysts, threat hunters, security engineers, architects.
- **IT operations** — impacted for endpoint, network, identity products; can veto on stability grounds.
- **Cloud / infrastructure teams** — for cloud security products.
- **Application / DevOps / engineering** — for AppSec, DevSecOps products; shift-left dynamics.
- **GRC / compliance** — for GRC, TPRM, audit tools.
- **Legal / privacy** — DPAs, data handling, breach notification obligations.
- **Procurement** — mature and formal in enterprise; security-specific vendor questionnaires common.
- **Executive team / Board** — for strategic purchases, often informed even if not deciding.

**Pattern:** engineers often evaluate; executives often decide; procurement often gates. Messaging and collateral must serve all three.

## 5. Trigger events

Compelling events that create urgency:

- **Breach / ransomware incident** — internal or peer company. Peer-company breaches drive a quarter of cyber purchases.
- **Cyber insurance renewal** — insurer demands specific controls (MFA, EDR, backup).
- **Audit or compliance finding** — SOC 2, PCI, HIPAA, HITRUST, or regulatory exam.
- **Regulatory change** — SEC cyber rules, NYDFS Part 500 update, EO directive, PCI 4.0, state privacy laws.
- **New CISO hire** — first 6 months typically includes vendor audit and stack changes.
- **M&A event** — integration security, TDR (technology due diligence), post-close consolidation.
- **Incumbent vendor failure** — CrowdStrike Falcon outage effect, SolarWinds fallout, Kaseya ransomware, supply chain incidents.
- **Cloud migration milestone** — AWS/Azure/GCP posture shift opens cloud security buying.
- **Ransomware / extortion incident** — drives backup, EDR, identity, segmentation.
- **Executive / board-level risk review** — often post-incident or post-news event.
- **New technology adoption** — GenAI use triggers data security and identity review; IoT/OT deployment triggers OT security.

## 6. Positioning — unique-attribute categories

Durable differentiation in cyber:

- **MITRE ATT&CK coverage & efficacy** — measured, published.
- **Independent lab results** — AV-TEST, AV-Comparatives, SE Labs, MITRE Engenuity ATT&CK Evaluations, NSS Labs (historical).
- **Analyst position** — Gartner MQ Leader/Challenger quadrant, Forrester Wave Leader.
- **Platform breadth vs. point excellence** — consolidation story vs. best-of-breed story.
- **Time to detect / time to respond** — specific metrics.
- **False positive rate** — quantified.
- **Deployment speed** — agent-based vs. agentless; time to first value.
- **Ease of operation** — SOC-ready outputs, automation depth, noise suppression.
- **Integration ecosystem** — named integrations with SIEM, SOAR, EDR, identity, ticketing, cloud.
- **Regulatory / compliance mapping** — CIS Controls, NIST CSF, ISO 27001, HIPAA, PCI, FedRAMP.
- **Deployment model** — SaaS, on-prem, hybrid, air-gapped, FedRAMP-authorized.
- **Economic story** — tool consolidation, insurance premium reduction, SOC headcount avoidance.

Positioning without third-party validation is weak in cyber. "We think we're great" loses to "Gartner named us Leader" every time.

## 7. Pricing & model patterns

- **Per user / per identity** — identity, MFA, security awareness.
- **Per endpoint** — EPP, EDR, MDR, DLP.
- **Per asset / per workload** — cloud security, CWPP, CSPM.
- **Per volume** — data ingested (SIEM), events processed, emails scanned.
- **Platform + modules** — common for consolidation plays; one platform contract with à la carte modules.
- **Managed service pricing** — MDR typically per endpoint + platform fee; MSSP typically per device or per event tier + retainer.
- **Annual / multi-year commits** — standard; 3-year deals common with escalators.
- **Enterprise agreements** — large-vendor EAs often bundle security (e.g., Microsoft E5) creating displacement economics for point tools.

Pricing strategy is ICP strategy: Microsoft E5-committed buyers are very hard for point vendors to displace without a clear delta; CrowdStrike Falcon-committed buyers same; Palo Alto Prisma same. Name these platform commitments in your ICP.

## 8. Compliance / regulatory overlay

Material for both vendor (as sellable posture) and buyer (as buying driver):

- **SOC 2 Type II** — universal baseline.
- **ISO 27001** — enterprise and international.
- **FedRAMP** — US federal; long and expensive path but unlocks federal TAM.
- **StateRAMP** — state/local government.
- **CMMC** — US Department of Defense supply chain.
- **PCI-DSS** — for payment-adjacent; various levels.
- **HITRUST** — healthcare.
- **HIPAA** — healthcare.
- **NYDFS Part 500** — NY-regulated financial.
- **SEC cyber rules** — public company disclosure.
- **EO 14028 + NIST SBOM guidance** — federal and supply chain.
- **DORA** (EU Digital Operational Resilience Act) — EU financial services.
- **NIS2** — EU cyber regulation.
- **GDPR breach notification** — 72-hour notification obligations.

For vendors selling to regulated buyers, the compliance kit is a gate. For vendors selling compliance tools, regulatory change is the single biggest demand driver.

## 9. Archetypal ICP slices

**A — "Mid-market, new CISO, consolidation-ready"**
US-based, $100M–$1B revenue, new CISO hired in last 12 months, currently running 40+ security tools with visible fatigue, stated consolidation mandate from CFO or CEO, cyber insurance renewal in next 6 months. Trigger: new CISO, insurance renewal, or M&A.

**B — "Enterprise, platform consolidation play"**
Enterprise ($1B+), committed to Microsoft E5 or similar bundle, point-solution fatigue, CISO with board visibility, 3–5 legacy tools up for renewal in next 12 months. Trigger: platform bundle purchase, multi-tool renewal cluster, or major incident.

**C — "Regulated vertical with compliance-driven buying"**
Healthcare / financial services / critical infrastructure, $250M+ revenue, recent audit finding or regulatory action, named compliance/CISO leader, explicit budget line for named compliance control. Trigger: audit/exam finding, new regulation, or breach notification obligation.

**D — "Mid-market MDR buyer"**
$50M–$250M revenue, IT-led security with 1–3 dedicated security staff, cannot staff 24/7 SOC, has EDR or considering it, cyber insurance pressure, willing to outsource monitoring. Trigger: insurance requirement, incident scare, or staff turnover.

**E — "Fast-growing SaaS with cloud-native security needs"**
$25M–$250M ARR cloud-native SaaS, born in AWS/GCP, engineering-led security posture, DevOps/DevSecOps culture, current tooling is either developer-friendly or absent, growing faster than security team. Trigger: customer security review (from their enterprise buyer), SOC 2 audit, or engineering-led pain.

## 10. Common failure modes

- **Feature-based positioning in a crowded category.** Commoditizes instantly.
- **Ignoring the CISO's career calculus.** CISOs buy what won't get them fired. Vendors who understand this close faster.
- **Missing the platform consolidation wave.** Point solutions without a consolidation story are walking into a headwind.
- **Under-investing in analyst & reference validation.** Cyber buying is reference-heavy; no Gartner recognition = hard-mode enterprise sales.
- **Under-resourcing MITRE / independent evals.** These are the credential that unlocks technical evaluators.
- **Selling to engineers without an exec story.** Engineers can kill deals but rarely close them.
- **Ignoring the insurance driver.** A meaningful share of cyber purchases is insurer-mandated; messaging should make the insurance story easy.
- **Overpromising detection.** Sets up post-purchase disillusionment and churn at renewal.
- **Channel / MSSP conflict.** For direct sellers, conflict with channel partners can strangle deals; for indirect, MSSP economics dictate ICP.
