# ICP — HealthTech Playbook

> Vertical playbook for companies selling into healthcare. Healthcare is a heavily regulated, fragmented, and politically-inflected market; this doc highlights the differences that ICP work must account for.

## 1. What makes healthcare distinct

Five structural features:

**Regulatory gravity is enormous.** HIPAA, state privacy laws, FDA (for medical devices and SaMD — Software as Medical Device), CMS rules, Stark Law, Anti-Kickback Statute, 21st Century Cures Act (information blocking), ONC certification, and an ocean of interpretation. The compliance burden is a primary ICP filter in both directions.

**Buyer fragmentation is extreme.** "Healthcare" includes health systems, hospitals, physician groups, health plans (payers), pharma/biotech, medical devices, life sciences, digital health vendors, retail pharmacy, long-term care, behavioral health, home health — each with different buying processes, timelines, and terminology.

**Sales cycles are punishing.** 9–24 months is typical for hospital and payer sales. Pilots that don't convert can last years. Deal velocity is structurally slower than almost any other category.

**Reimbursement dictates economics.** Many buying decisions are downstream of reimbursement codes (CPT, HCPCS, DRG) and payer coverage decisions. Products without a reimbursement path have a much smaller ICP.

**Reference and peer influence are overwhelming.** Health systems and physicians trust each other far more than they trust vendors. A KOL endorsement, peer-reviewed publication, or same-specialty customer reference is worth more than any marketing asset.

## 2. Healthcare is many markets

Primary buyer subdivisions (treat as different ICPs):

**Providers:**
- **Academic medical centers (AMCs)** — large, research-oriented, innovation-friendly but slow.
- **Integrated Delivery Networks (IDNs)** — multi-hospital systems.
- **Community hospitals** — mid-size; slower tech adoption.
- **Critical access / rural hospitals** — budget-constrained.
- **Physician groups / independent practices** — different buying process than hospitals.
- **FQHCs / community health centers** — federal funding, specific buying rules.
- **Urgent care / retail clinics**.
- **Behavioral health providers**.
- **Long-term care / SNFs / senior living**.
- **Home health / hospice**.

**Payers:**
- **National health plans** (UnitedHealth, Anthem/Elevance, Aetna/CVS, Cigna, Humana, BCBS plans).
- **Regional / Blues plans**.
- **Medicare Advantage plans**.
- **Medicaid Managed Care Organizations (MCOs)**.
- **Employer self-funded / TPAs**.
- **Pharmacy Benefit Managers (PBMs)**.

**Life sciences / pharma:**
- **Big Pharma / biotech** — commercial, medical affairs, R&D, clinical ops, patient services.
- **Emerging biotech**.
- **CROs** (Contract Research Organizations).
- **Medical device companies**.

**Other:**
- **Digital health companies** (as buyers of infrastructure).
- **Health IT / EHR vendors**.
- **Public health agencies**.
- **Employers** (for point-solution benefits purchases).

## 3. ICP patterns

Useful dimensions:

- **Buyer type** (per Section 2).
- **Size**: for providers, bed count / revenue / NPI count / lives covered. For payers, lives covered / premium volume. For pharma, therapeutic area + stage.
- **EHR / core system**: Epic, Cerner (now Oracle Health), Meditech, Athenahealth, eClinicalWorks, NextGen, Allscripts (now Veradigm), Greenway — dictates integration path.
- **Reimbursement exposure**: fee-for-service vs. value-based care / risk-bearing; mix of Medicare/Medicaid/commercial.
- **Clinical specialty**: many products are specialty-specific; ICP must name the specialty.
- **Regulatory posture**: HIPAA / HITRUST, any specific accreditations (TJC, NCQA, URAC, AAAHC), SOC 2 requirement.
- **Technology maturity**: HIMSS EMRAM stage, cloud posture, data warehouse/lake status, FHIR/API posture.
- **Decision authority**: hospital-level vs. system-level; medical group vs. administrative authority; IT-led vs. clinical-led vs. CMO-led.

## 4. The buying committee

Healthcare committees are notoriously large. Typical provider-side composite:

- **Economic buyer** — CFO, COO, CMO (Chief Medical Officer), CIO, CMIO (Chief Medical Information Officer), Chief Quality Officer, Chief Nursing Officer, depending on product type.
- **Champion** — Service line leader, department chief, medical director, VP Quality, VP Clinical Operations.
- **End users** — physicians, nurses, technicians, allied health. Clinical adoption can kill or save the deal.
- **IT / informatics** — CIO, CMIO, HIT director, integration team.
- **Information security** — HIPAA Security Officer, CISO.
- **Privacy** — HIPAA Privacy Officer.
- **Compliance / regulatory** — General Counsel, Chief Compliance Officer.
- **Legal** — contracts, BAA, liability.
- **Supply chain / materials management** — formal procurement, GPO relationships.
- **Finance / reimbursement** — CFO, VP Revenue Cycle.
- **Value analysis committee (VAC)** — many hospitals route purchases through a multi-stakeholder committee.
- **Board / executive committee** — for strategic or high-cost purchases.
- **GPO relationship** — Vizient, Premier, HealthTrust, Intalere — affect pricing and vendor access.

Payer-side:
- **Economic buyer** — CMO, CIO, COO, Chief Actuary, VP Clinical.
- **Champion** — Medical Director, Head of Care Management, Head of Quality, Head of Network.
- **Clinical evaluation** — utilization management, population health.
- **IT / architecture** — claims integration, data lake, API.
- **Compliance, privacy, legal, procurement** — as with providers.

Pharma/life sciences: different entirely — decisions by therapeutic area, commercial brand team, medical affairs, clinical operations, or IT, depending on product. Names vary by company.

## 5. Trigger events

Healthcare-specific compelling events:

- **New C-suite hire** (CMO, CMIO, CIO, CMO, CFO, CNO) — first 6–12 months.
- **EHR implementation or upgrade** — Epic/Oracle Health/Meditech projects open entire adjacent vendor categories.
- **Reimbursement change** — new CPT code, Medicare coverage decision, value-based care contract, bundled payment program.
- **Regulatory change** — CMS rule change, ONC certification update, HIPAA enforcement, FDA guidance.
- **Quality / accreditation event** — Joint Commission, NCQA, URAC survey findings.
- **Merger / acquisition** — system consolidation, practice acquisition, payer merger.
- **Financial pressure** — margin compression drives revenue cycle and cost-reduction buying.
- **Clinical event** — sentinel event, never event, quality metric miss.
- **Population health / VBC initiative** — entry into risk-bearing arrangements drives analytics and care management buying.
- **Cybersecurity incident** — healthcare is a top ransomware target; breaches trigger rebuilds.
- **Workforce crisis** — nurse shortage, physician burnout drives automation, clinical decision support, ambient documentation.
- **New payer contract or product launch** — new Medicare Advantage plan, new commercial product.

## 6. Positioning — unique-attribute categories

Durable differentiation in healthtech:

- **Clinical evidence** — peer-reviewed publications, outcome data, real-world evidence.
- **Regulatory / certification status** — FDA clearance (510(k), De Novo, PMA), FDA breakthrough designation, ONC certification, HITRUST certification, HIPAA compliance attestation.
- **Reimbursement pathway** — existing CPT/HCPCS code, AMA CPT category pathway, payer coverage policies.
- **EHR integration depth** — Epic App Orchard, Oracle Health (Cerner) integration, Athena Marketplace; native SMART-on-FHIR; bidirectional vs. read-only.
- **Interoperability** — FHIR API maturity, HL7 support, USCDI coverage.
- **Specialty / workflow specificity** — built for a specific specialty, care setting, or workflow.
- **Outcome metrics** — clinical (readmissions, HEDIS, STAR, MIPS), financial (days in AR, denial rate, cost per case), operational (throughput, wait time).
- **KOL endorsements** — physician champions with credibility in the specialty.
- **Reference customers** — named, specialty-matched, willing to be visited.
- **Workflow fit** — saves clinician time vs. adds burden (a critical differentiation angle given physician burnout).

"Best-in-class AI" positioning without clinical evidence, reimbursement pathway, or EHR integration doesn't move healthcare buyers.

## 7. Pricing & model patterns

- **Per provider / per clinician** — common for clinical tools.
- **Per bed / per hospital** — for hospital platforms.
- **Per member per month (PMPM)** — payer and VBC contracts.
- **Per encounter / per visit** — transactional.
- **Per claim / per case** — revenue cycle, utilization management.
- **Shared savings / gainshare** — value-based pricing aligned to outcomes.
- **Capitated models** — for population health.
- **Enterprise / subscription** — system-level platform deals.
- **Implementation services** — often substantial; can exceed first-year license.
- **GPO contracts** — participation in group purchasing can be a prerequisite.

Pricing must match how the buyer budgets. Hospitals budget differently than physician groups; payers differently than pharma; each requires different pricing mechanics.

## 8. Compliance / regulatory overlay

Essentials:

- **HIPAA** — Privacy Rule, Security Rule, Breach Notification. BAA (Business Associate Agreement) required for any PHI touching.
- **HITRUST CSF** — the de facto healthcare security/compliance framework.
- **SOC 2 Type II** — baseline.
- **FDA** — for clinical software that qualifies as SaMD, Software in a Medical Device, or companion diagnostics. 510(k), De Novo, PMA pathways.
- **ONC certification** — for EHR and EHR-adjacent products.
- **21st Century Cures Act** — information blocking, interoperability requirements.
- **State privacy laws** — California CMIA, Washington My Health My Data, Texas, Connecticut, others.
- **42 CFR Part 2** — substance use disorder records (separate and stricter than HIPAA).
- **CLIA** — clinical laboratory.
- **Stark Law, Anti-Kickback Statute (AKS)** — affect pricing, discounts, and referral-related structures.
- **Medicare / Medicaid integrity** — false claims, overpayment risk.
- **GDPR / international** — for cross-border data.
- **FedRAMP / HITRUST Government** — for federal health agency buyers (VA, DoD, HHS, CMS).

Validation burden for FDA-regulated products is significant: design controls, clinical validation, post-market surveillance, adverse event reporting.

## 9. Archetypal ICP slices

**A — "Large IDN with Epic, clinical workflow buyer"**
US-based IDN, 3+ hospitals / 10+ locations, Epic-powered, has named CMIO with 2+ years tenure, currently using or considering ambient AI/clinical documentation/specific workflow tool, has published innovation initiative, participates in Epic user community. Trigger: EHR upgrade, physician burnout initiative, or service line launch.

**B — "Mid-size Medicare Advantage plan, care management buyer"**
Regional MA plan, 100k–1M lives, has named Chief Medical Officer or VP Care Management, managing star ratings and HEDIS measures actively, participates in CMS value-based care programs. Trigger: star rating drop, new CMS rule, or network expansion.

**C — "Pharma commercial team, patient services buyer"**
Mid-large pharma, specific therapeutic area team (rare disease / oncology / immunology / specialty), has Patient Services or Market Access team, launching or supporting a branded therapy with access challenges. Trigger: new product launch, coverage/access issue, or patient support program expansion.

**D — "Physician group with value-based care exposure"**
Mid-size physician group, 50–500 providers, entered or expanding into risk-bearing contracts (ACO, MA, direct contracting), needs analytics / care coordination / quality measurement support. Trigger: new VBC contract, quality metric miss, or practice acquisition.

**E — "Digital health vendor buying infrastructure"**
Series B/C digital health company, $10M–$50M revenue, building on cloud, needs HITRUST/SOC 2/HIPAA-compliant infrastructure, scaling rapidly, engineering-led decisions. Trigger: customer security review, audit cycle, or infrastructure scaling pain.

## 10. Common failure modes

- **Treating "healthcare" as one market.** Providers, payers, pharma are different businesses.
- **Ignoring EHR integration.** Products that don't integrate with Epic/Oracle Health are non-starters at most provider accounts.
- **Under-investing in clinical evidence.** Without peer-reviewed outcomes, KOL endorsement, or published case studies, technical evaluators won't advance the deal.
- **Under-resourcing the sales cycle.** 9–24 months is the floor. Cash-flow planning must reflect this.
- **Missing the reimbursement question.** If there's no payment path for the service your product enables, the ICP is narrower than you think.
- **Pitching "innovation" to risk-averse buyers.** Healthcare buyers optimize for "will this work and who else has done it."
- **Ignoring GPO / purchasing organization gatekeepers.** Vizient, Premier, HealthTrust contracts gate access to many systems.
- **Overlooking workflow integration.** A product that adds 2 minutes to clinician workflow will be abandoned even if it improves outcomes.
- **Missing information blocking / Cures Act compliance implications.** Affects data sharing, API exposure, and customer obligations.
- **Underestimating post-sale complexity.** Healthcare customers require more services and longer onboarding than typical SaaS.
